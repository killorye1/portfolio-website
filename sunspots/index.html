More on sunspots

  • Sunspots are cooler areas on the Sun’s photosphere
  • They appear dark by contrast with their surroundings
  • They come in North and South pairs

Research Question:

Are sunspots more likely to appear at certain times of the year?

Hypothesis:

We believe that solar activity increases during the autumn months.


Procedure

We used www.spaceweather.com to collect data on the numbers of sunspots occuring on the 15th of each month for 10 years from January 2008 to December 2017

We took the averages of the amount of sunspots occuring on the 15th of each of the 12 months across the 10 years

We calculated the average for all of the months combined so we could determine which months, on average, experienced an above average number of sunspots. The number determining the difference between below average and above average was 57.65

Conclusion:

From the data gathered we detected we observed the average number of sunspots were above average during the months of May and November for this sunspot cycle. Other inferences we could make from the research is that sunspots occur in a cycle reaching highs and lows spanning up to multiple decades.

Our sun is currently in its 11th sunspot cycle. Although we noticed a spike in activity around the months of May and November, ten years is a small sample size in the context of the universe and more data would need to be gathered to further support our hypothesis.
